What is Software Engineering?

More Than Just Coding

[cite_start]Software engineering is an engineering discipline that is concerned with all aspects of software production[cite: 40]. [cite_start]This goes far beyond just programming and includes activities like project management, requirements gathering, and system maintenance[cite: 36, 37]. [cite_start]The goal is to produce reliable and trustworthy systems economically and quickly[cite: 28].

Analogy: Think of building a house. A programmer is like a carpenter who is skilled at construction. A software engineer is like the architect and general contractor who designs the blueprint, manages the project, ensures it meets building codes, and oversees the entire process from foundation to finish.

How it Differs from Related Fields

    [cite_start]
  • Computer Science: Focuses on theory and the fundamental principles of computing[cite: 40]. [cite_start]Software engineering is more concerned with the practicalities of developing and delivering useful software[cite: 40].
    • [cite_start]
  • System Engineering: Is a broader field that is concerned with all aspects of developing computer-based systems, including hardware, software, and processes[cite: 40]. [cite_start]Software engineering is one part of this larger process[cite: 40].

Generic vs. Customized Software

Software products can be broadly categorized into two types based on who they are developed for and who controls their direction.

Generic Products

[cite_start]

These are stand-alone systems sold to any customer on the open market[cite: 9, 10]. Think of Microsoft Office or Adobe Photoshop.

    [cite_start]
  • Specification is owned by the **developer**[cite: 12].
    • [cite_start]
  • Change decisions are made by the **developer**[cite: 12].

Customized Products

[cite_start]

This is software commissioned by a specific client for their own needs[cite: 14]. Think of an air traffic control system or software for a specific factory robot.

    [cite_start]
  • Specification is owned by the **customer**[cite: 16].
    • [cite_start]
  • Change decisions are made by the **customer**[cite: 17].

Attributes of Good Software

Good software is more than just functional. [cite_start]It must also possess several key characteristics that determine its quality and long-term value[cite: 40].

  • [cite_start]
    Maintainability: Software must be able to evolve to meet changing needs[cite: 43]. [cite_start]This is critical because change is inevitable in business[cite: 43].
  • [cite_start]
    Dependability and Security: Software should not cause economic or physical damage upon failure and must be protected from unauthorized access[cite: 43].
  • [cite_start]
    Efficiency: Software should make good use of system resources like memory and processor cycles, ensuring fast response times[cite: 43].
  • [cite_start]
    Acceptability: Software must be usable, understandable, and compatible with other systems for its target users[cite: 43].

Software Engineering Ethics

Professional Responsibility

[cite_start]

Software engineering involves more than just technical skills; it requires behaving in an honest and ethically responsible manner[cite: 126, 127]. [cite_start]This is crucial for being respected as a professional[cite: 127]. [cite_start]Ethical behavior goes beyond just following the law; it means adhering to morally correct principles[cite: 128].

    [cite_start]
  • Confidentiality: Engineers must respect the confidentiality of their employers and clients, even without a formal agreement[cite: 132].
    • [cite_start]
  • Competence: Engineers should not misrepresent their skill level or knowingly accept work they are not competent to perform[cite: 134].
    • [cite_start]
  • Intellectual Property: Engineers must be aware of and protect intellectual property rights, such as patents and copyrights[cite: 138].
    • [cite_start]
  • Computer Misuse: Software engineers must not use their technical skills to misuse other people's computers, which can range from trivial acts to serious offenses like spreading viruses[cite: 140, 141].
